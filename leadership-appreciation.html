Yet more always comes.
The days grow longer and tired,
but when did the night appear so quickly?
Though we still descend,
splintered buckets reaching to drink from their own wells.

But a longer rope, a brighter morning
held by those who give, then given.
Bring forth cups of water or coffee or beer
now pressed to our mouths; shake off the fading year.
Take us on through night and Winter and newer storms,
and even striving within the smallest moments of laughter or grief.

This gratitude begins in words, the little signs of changing seasons—
the first cooler breeze bearing orange banners,
the first flash of green in March.
They’ll continue growing into something that you will always know
arrives with the same steadfast certainty
as the more that always comes.

~ Gia Gianakis ~
